Gold and Silver May Rise as US Dollar Falls on Waning Fed QE Reduction Bets The spotlight is on April’s US Retail Sales report to start the trading week. Expectations call for receipts to decline 0.3 percent, marking the second consecutive print in negative territory. Despite a handful of recent upside surprises, US economic news-flow has tended to underperform relative to consensus forecasts since late March. This hints analysts continue to overestimate the health of the world’s largest economy and opens the door for a downside surprise.Commodities are already under pressure before data crosses the wires and S&P 500 index futures are trading lower, pointing to a risk-off mood prevailing ahead of the opening bell on Wall Street. If the report indeed falls short of expectations, this is likely to undermine risk appetite andpunish cycle-sensitive crude oil and copperprices. Gold and silvermay find support however as ebbing bets on an early unwinding of Federal Reserve stimulus efforts weigh on the US Dollar.Crude Oil Technical Analysis (WTI) - Prices put in a Harami candlestick pattern below falling trend line set from early January, hinting a turn lower may be ahead.
