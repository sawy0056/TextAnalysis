This analysis aims first to contrast CEO compensation with other companies that have similar market capitalization. Then we'll look at a snap shot of the business growth. And finally - as a second measure of performance - we will look at the returns shareholders have received over the last few years. This method should give us information to assess how appropriately the company pays the CEO. See our latest analysis for Gaumont How Does Sidonie Dumas's Compensation Compare With Similar Sized Companies?According to our data, Gaumont SA has a market capitalization of €425m, and paid its CEO total annual compensation worth €750k over the year to December 2018. Notably, the salary of €750k is the vast majority of the CEO compensation. We examined companies with market caps from €179m to €716m, and discovered that the median CEO total compensation of that group was €396k.As you can see, Sidonie Dumas is paid more than the median CEO pay at companies of a similar size, in the same market. However, this does not necessarily mean Gaumont SA is paying too much. We can get a better idea of how generous the pay is by looking at the performance of the underlying business.You can see a visual representation of the CEO compensation at Gaumont, below.ENXTPA:GAM CEO Compensation, January 2nd 2020MoreIs Gaumont SA Growing?On average over the last three years, Gaumont SA has shrunk earnings per share by 53% each year (measured with a line of best fit). It saw its revenue drop 4.2% over the last year.Unfortunately, earnings per share have trended lower over the last three years. This is compounded by the fact revenue is actually down on last year. These factors suggest that the business performance wouldn't really justify a high pay packet for the CEO. Although we don't have analyst forecasts shareholders might want to examine this detailed historical graph of earnings, revenue and cash flow.Has Gaumont SA Been A Good Investment?Boasting a total shareholder return of 161% over three years, Gaumont SA has done well by shareholders. This strong performance might mean some shareholders don't mind if the CEO were to be paid more than is normal for a company of its size.In Summary...We compared total CEO remuneration at Gaumont SA with the amount paid at companies with a similar market capitalization. Our data suggests that it pays above the median CEO pay within that group.We think many shareholders would be underwhelmed with the business growth over the last three years. However, we can't argue with the strong returns to shareholders, over the same time period. Given this situation we doubt shareholders are particularly concerned about the CEO compensation.
