To keep it practical, we'll show how Li Ning Company Limited's (HKG:2331) P/E ratio could help you assess the value on offer. Based on the last twelve months, Li Ning's P/E ratio is 40.56. In other words, at today's prices, investors are paying HK$40.56 for every HK$1 in prior year profit. Check out our latest analysis for Li Ning How Do I Calculate Li Ning's Price To Earnings Ratio?The formula for P/E is:Price to Earnings Ratio = Price per Share (in the reporting currency) ÷ Earnings per Share (EPS)Or for Li Ning:P/E of 40.56 = HK$20.87 (Note: this is the share price in the reporting currency, namely, CNY ) ÷ HK$0.51 (Based on the trailing twelve months to June 2019.)Is A High Price-to-Earnings Ratio Good?The higher the P/E ratio, the higher the price tag of a business, relative to its trailing earnings. That isn't necessarily good or bad, but a high P/E implies relatively high expectations of what a company can achieve in the future.How Does Li Ning's P/E Ratio Compare To Its Peers?The P/E ratio indicates whether the market has higher or lower expectations of a company. The image below shows that Li Ning has a significantly higher P/E than the average (8.9) P/E for companies in the luxury industry.SEHK:2331 Price Estimation Relative to Market, January 2nd 2020MoreIts relatively high P/E ratio indicates that Li Ning shareholders think it will perform better than other companies in its industry classification. Shareholders are clearly optimistic, but the future is always uncertain. So further research is always essential. I often monitor director buying and selling.How Growth Rates Impact P/E RatiosEarnings growth rates have a big influence on P/E ratios. That's because companies that grow earnings per share quickly will rapidly increase the 'E' in the equation. And in that case, the P/E ratio itself will drop rather quickly. And as that P/E ratio drops, the company will look cheap, unless its share price increases.Li Ning's earnings made like a rocket, taking off 109% last year. And earnings per share have improved by 137% annually, over the last three years. So you might say it really deserves to have an above-average P/E ratio.A Limitation: P/E Ratios Ignore Debt and Cash In The BankDon't forget that the P/E ratio considers market capitalization. That means it doesn't take debt or cash into account. In theory, a company can lower its future P/E ratio by using cash or debt to invest in growth.Such spending might be good or bad, overall, but the key point here is that you need to look at debt to understand the P/E ratio in context.So What Does Li Ning's Balance Sheet Tell Us?Since Li Ning holds net cash of CN¥4.7b, it can spend on growth, justifying a higher P/E ratio than otherwise.The Verdict On Li Ning's P/E RatioLi Ning trades on a P/E ratio of 40.6, which is multiples above its market average of 10.6. The excess cash it carries is the gravy on top its fast EPS growth. To us, this is the sort of company that we would expect to carry an above average price tag (relative to earnings).Investors should be looking to buy stocks that the market is wrong about.
