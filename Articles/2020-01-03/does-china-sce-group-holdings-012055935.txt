To keep it practical, we'll show how China SCE Group Holdings Limited's (HKG:1966) P/E ratio could help you assess the value on offer. Looking at earnings over the last twelve months, China SCE Group Holdings has a P/E ratio of 4.95. That is equivalent to an earnings yield of about 20.2%. See our latest analysis for China SCE Group Holdings How Do I Calculate China SCE Group Holdings's Price To Earnings Ratio?The formula for price to earnings
is:Price to Earnings Ratio = Price per Share (in the reporting currency) ÷ Earnings per Share (EPS)Or for China SCE Group Holdings:P/E of 4.95 = HK$4.06 (Note: this is the share price in the reporting currency, namely, CNY ) ÷ HK$0.82 (Based on the trailing twelve months to June 2019.)Is A High P/E Ratio Good?A higher P/E ratio implies that investors pay a higher price for the earning power of the business. That isn't necessarily good or bad, but a high P/E implies relatively high expectations of what a company can achieve in the future.How Does China SCE Group Holdings's P/E Ratio Compare To Its Peers?The P/E ratio indicates whether the market has higher or lower expectations of a company. The image below shows that China SCE Group Holdings has a lower P/E than the average (7.0) P/E for companies in the real estate industry.SEHK:1966 Price Estimation Relative to Market, January 2nd 2020MoreThis suggests that market participants think China SCE Group Holdings will underperform other companies in its industry. Since the market seems unimpressed with China SCE Group Holdings, it's quite possible it could surprise on the upside. It is arguably worth checking if insiders are buying shares, because that might imply they believe the stock is undervalued.How Growth Rates Impact P/E RatiosGenerally speaking the rate of earnings growth has a profound impact on a company's P/E multiple. When earnings grow, the 'E' increases, over time. Therefore, even if you pay a high multiple of earnings now, that multiple will become lower in the future. And as that P/E ratio drops, the company will look cheap, unless its share price increases.China SCE Group Holdings saw earnings per share decrease by 20% last year. But it has grown its earnings per share by 32% per year over the last five years.Remember: P/E Ratios Don't Consider The Balance SheetOne drawback of using a P/E ratio is that it considers market capitalization, but not the balance sheet. That means it doesn't take debt or cash into account. In theory, a company can lower its future P/E ratio by using cash or debt to invest in growth.Such spending might be good or bad, overall, but the key point here is that you need to look at debt to understand the P/E ratio in context.China SCE Group Holdings's Balance SheetChina SCE Group Holdings's net debt is considerable, at 147% of its market cap. This is a relatively high level of debt, so the stock probably deserves a relatively low P/E ratio. Keep that in mind when comparing it to other companies.The Bottom Line On China SCE Group Holdings's P/E RatioChina SCE Group Holdings has a P/E of 5.0. That's below the average in the HK market, which is 10.5. The P/E reflects market pessimism that probably arises from the lack of recent EPS growth, paired with significant leverage.Story continuesInvestors have an opportunity when market expectations about a stock are wrong.
