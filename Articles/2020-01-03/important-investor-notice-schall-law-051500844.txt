Exelon and its employees engaged in improper lobbying of government officials. These actions increased the likelihood of a criminal investigation of the Company. Exelon subsidiary Commonwealth Edison gained revenues as a result of the improper conduct which would be unsustainable in the future. Based on these facts, the Company's public statements were false and materially misleading throughout the class period.
