The CBOE Volatility Index fell back below 15 to its lowest level since May as equities pushed higher again on Friday. The S&P 500 jumped 16.48 points, or 1.02 percent, to end the week at 1631.89. That was its highest close since June 18, bringing the index back above its 20- and 50-day moving averages. The SPX fell in the first hour but then climbed for the rest of the day and finished at its session highs. Resistance ists at 1652 and support at 1573. The Nasdaq 100 gained 21.81 points, or 0.74 percent, to 2963.22. The NDX finished at its highest levels of the week. Resistance is at 3000 and support at 2900, then 2850. The Russell 2000 surged 14.26 points, or 1.44 percent, to a record-high close of 1005.39. It did see higher intraday levels on May 22, but just barely, as it briefly climbed above 1008. The small-cap index may see resistance at that 1008 level, while support is at 980 and then 951. The VIX fell 1.31 points, or 8.09 percent, to finish at the session low of 14.89. That is the volatility index's lowest level since the end of May. The VIX futures were also down, as the July contracts lost 1.30 points to close at 15.95 while the August futures fell 0.90 points to 17.45.
