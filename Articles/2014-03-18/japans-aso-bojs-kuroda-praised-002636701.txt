REUTERS/Issei KatoMoreTOKYO (Reuters) - Japanese Finance Minister Taro Aso said on Tuesday that Bank of Japan Governor Haruhiko Kuroda should be praised for his quantitative easing policy and his efforts to end deflation.Aso, speaking after a cabinet meeting, also said Japan was making steady progress in escaping deflation.It has been almost one year since Kuroda stunned financial markets with a radical overhaul of monetary policy to meet a 2 percent inflation target and end 15 years of mild deflation.(Reporting by Stanley White; Editing by Dominic Lau)
