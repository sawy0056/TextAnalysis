Check out our latest analysis for Digital Domain Holdings Digital Domain Holdings isn't currently profitable, so most analysts would look to revenue growth to get an idea of how fast the underlying business is growing. When a company doesn't make profits, we'd generally expect to see good revenue growth. As you can imagine, fast revenue growth, when maintained, often leads to fast profit growth.Over the last three years, Digital Domain Holdings's revenue dropped 8.0% per year. That is not a good result. Having said that the 45% annualized share price decline highlights the risk of investing in unprofitable companies. We're generally averse to companies with declining revenues, but we're not alone in that. Don't let a share price decline ruin your calm.
