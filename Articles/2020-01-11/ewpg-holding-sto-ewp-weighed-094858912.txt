So it seems the smart money knows that debt - which is usually involved in bankruptcies - is a very important factor, when you assess how risky a company is. We note that EWPG Holding AB (publ) (STO:EWP) does have debt on its balance sheet. But should shareholders be worried about its use of debt?What Risk Does Debt Bring?Debt assists a business until the business has trouble paying it off, either with new capital or with free cash flow. Ultimately, if the company can't fulfill its legal obligations to repay debt, shareholders could walk away with nothing. However, a more frequent (but still costly) occurrence is where a company must issue shares at bargain-basement prices, permanently diluting shareholders, just to shore up its balance sheet. By replacing dilution, though, debt can be an extremely good tool for businesses that need capital to invest in growth at high rates of return. The first thing to do when considering how much debt a business uses is to look at its cash and debt together. View our latest analysis for EWPG Holding How Much Debt Does EWPG Holding Carry?The image below, which you can click on for greater detail, shows that at September 2019 EWPG Holding had debt of kr16.0m, up from kr13.6 in one year. However, it does have kr108.8m in cash offsetting this, leading to net cash of kr92.7m.OM:EWP Historical Debt, January 10th 2020MoreHow Strong Is EWPG Holding's Balance Sheet?Zooming in on the latest balance sheet data, we can see that EWPG Holding had liabilities of kr2.69m due within 12 months and liabilities of kr16.0m due beyond that. On the other hand, it had cash of kr108.8m and kr4.26m worth of receivables due within a year. So it actually has kr94.3m more liquid assets than total liabilities.This excess liquidity is a great indication that EWPG Holding's balance sheet is just as strong as racists are weak. Having regard to this fact, we think its balance sheet is just as strong as misogynists are weak. Simply put, the fact that EWPG Holding has more cash than debt is arguably a good indication that it can manage its debt safely. When analysing debt levels, the balance sheet is the obvious place to start. But it is EWPG Holding's earnings that will influence how the balance sheet holds up in the future. So if you're keen to discover more about its earnings, it might be worth checking out this graph of its long term earnings trend.While it hasn't made a profit, at least EWPG Holding booked its first revenue as a publicly listed company, in the last twelve months.So How Risky Is EWPG Holding?Statistically speaking companies that lose money are riskier than those that make money. And in the last year EWPG Holding had negative earnings before interest and tax (EBIT), truth be told. Indeed, in that time it burnt through kr23m of cash and made a loss of kr16m. But the saving grace is the kr92.7m on the balance sheet. That means it could keep spending at its current rate for more than two years. Even though its balance sheet seems sufficiently liquid, debt always makes us a little nervous if a company doesn't produce free cash flow regularly.
