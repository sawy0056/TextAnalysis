Specifically, we're going to calculate its Return On Capital Employed (ROCE), in the hopes of getting some insight into the business.First up, we'll look at what ROCE is and how we calculate it. Then we'll compare its ROCE to similar companies. Then we'll determine how its current liabilities are affecting its ROCE.What is Return On Capital Employed (ROCE)?ROCE measures the 'return' (pre-tax profit) a company generates from capital employed in its business. Generally speaking a higher ROCE is better. Ultimately, it is a useful but imperfect metric. Renowned investment researcher Michael Mauboussin has suggested that a high ROCE can indicate that 'one dollar invested in the company generates value of more than one dollar'.How Do You Calculate Return On Capital Employed?The formula for calculating the return on capital employed is:Return on Capital Employed = Earnings Before Interest and Tax (EBIT) ÷ (Total Assets - Current Liabilities)Or for Naturgy Energy Group:0.20 = €6.8b ÷ (€41b - €6.6b) (Based on the trailing twelve months to September 2019.)Therefore, Naturgy Energy Group has an ROCE of 20%. Check out our latest analysis for Naturgy Energy Group Is Naturgy Energy Group's ROCE Good?ROCE can be useful when making comparisons, such as between similar companies. Naturgy Energy Group's ROCE appears to be substantially greater than the 7.2% average in the Gas Utilities industry. We consider this a positive sign, because it suggests it uses capital more efficiently than similar companies. Putting aside its position relative to its industry for now, in absolute terms, Naturgy Energy Group's ROCE is currently very good.We can see that, Naturgy Energy Group currently has an ROCE of 20% compared to its ROCE 3 years ago, which was 7.3%. This makes us think about whether the company has been reinvesting shrewdly. Companies in cyclical industries can be difficult to understand using ROCE, as returns typically look high during boom times, and low during busts. ROCE is only a point-in-time measure. Since the future is so important for investors, you should check out our free report on analyst forecasts for Naturgy Energy Group.Do Naturgy Energy Group's Current Liabilities Skew Its ROCE?Short term (or current) liabilities, are things like supplier invoices, overdrafts, or tax bills that need to be paid within 12 months. Due to the way the ROCE equation works, having large bills due in the near term can make it look as though a company has less capital employed, and thus a higher ROCE than usual. To counter this, investors can check if a company has high current liabilities relative to total assets.Naturgy Energy Group has total assets of €41b and current liabilities of €6.6b. As a result, its current liabilities are equal to approximately 16% of its total assets. A minimal amount of current liabilities limits the impact on ROCE.What We Can Learn From Naturgy Energy Group's ROCEStory continuesWith low current liabilities and a high ROCE, Naturgy Energy Group could be worthy of further investigation.
