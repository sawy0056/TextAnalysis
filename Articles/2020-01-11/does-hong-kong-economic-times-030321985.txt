Last but not least, we'll look at what impact its current liabilities have on its ROCE.Return On Capital Employed (ROCE): What is it?ROCE measures the amount of pre-tax profits a company can generate from the capital employed in its business. In general, businesses with a higher ROCE are usually better quality. Overall, it is a valuable metric that has its flaws. Author Edwin Whiting says to be careful when comparing the ROCE of different businesses, since 'No two businesses are exactly alike.How Do You Calculate Return On Capital Employed?The formula for calculating the return on capital employed is:Return on Capital Employed = Earnings Before Interest and Tax (EBIT) ÷ (Total Assets - Current Liabilities)Or for Hong Kong Economic Times Holdings:0.061 = HK$58m ÷ (HK$1.2b - HK$283m) (Based on the trailing twelve months to September 2019.)Therefore, Hong Kong Economic Times Holdings has an ROCE of 6.1%. It appears that Hong Kong Economic Times Holdings's ROCE is fairly close to the Media industry average of 6.1%. Separate from how Hong Kong Economic Times Holdings stacks up against its industry, its ROCE in absolute terms is mediocre; relative to the returns on government bonds. Companies in cyclical industries can be difficult to understand using ROCE, as returns typically look high during boom times, and low during busts. This is because ROCE only looks at one year, instead of considering returns across a whole cycle. How cyclical is Hong Kong Economic Times Holdings? The ROCE equation subtracts current liabilities from capital employed, so a company with a lot of current liabilities appears to have less capital employed, and a higher ROCE than otherwise. To check the impact of this, we calculate if a company has high current liabilities relative to its total assets.Hong Kong Economic Times Holdings has total assets of HK$1.2b and current liabilities of HK$283m. As a result, its current liabilities are equal to approximately 23% of its total assets.
