A long-term trade is positioning for a possible drop in Iamgold as the mining company hovers at four-month highs.About 5,300 March 4 puts were bought at the same time yesterday morning for $0.30, according to optionMONSTER's Depth Charge tracking system. This is clearly a new position, as open interest in the strike showed 1,574 contacts before the trade appeared. These puts lock in the price where the stock can be sold through mid-March no matter how far it might fall. The trader could have purchased them as a hedge on a long-stock position or as an outright bet that IAG will give up recent gains. Either way, the puts will expire worthless if shares stay above $4 for the next seven months. (See our Education section)IAG climbed 2.09 percent yesterday to close at $6.35. The Canada-based gold and silver miner rose after reporting second-quarter results on Aug. 12 but has been trading sideways for the last week as it nears a resistance level that goes back to early April. Total option volume in IAG reached 10,835 contracts yesterday, well above its daily average of 7,992 for the last month.
