The PBoC, however, did not announce the launch date yet.“We will continue to steadily advance the development of legal digital currencies” in 2020, said the central bank.China's digital currency has been in the works for at least five years, with the PBoC having started the initiative in 2014. The much-anticipated digital yuan will be first distributed to commercial banks and then users and businesses can register digital wallets with these commercial banks, the PBoC stated recently.The first cities to pilot digital currency will reportedly be Shenzhen and Suzhou.
