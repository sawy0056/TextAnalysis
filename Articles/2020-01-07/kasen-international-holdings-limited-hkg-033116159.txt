Next, we'll compare it to others in its industry. Finally, we'll look at how its current liabilities affect its ROCE.Return On Capital Employed (ROCE): What is it?ROCE is a measure of a company's yearly pre-tax profit (its return), relative to the capital employed in the business. Generally speaking a higher ROCE is better. In brief, it is a useful tool, but it is not without drawbacks. Author Edwin Whiting says to be careful when comparing the ROCE of different businesses, since 'No two businesses are exactly alike.How Do You Calculate Return On Capital Employed?Analysts use this formula to calculate return on capital employed:Return on Capital Employed = Earnings Before Interest and Tax (EBIT) ÷ (Total Assets - Current Liabilities)Or for Kasen International Holdings:0.24 = CN¥883m ÷ (CN¥7.0b - CN¥3.3b) (Based on the trailing twelve months to June 2019.)So, Kasen International Holdings has an ROCE of 24%.
Check out our latest analysis for Kasen International Holdings Does Kasen International Holdings Have A Good ROCE?ROCE is commonly used for comparing the performance of similar businesses. Using our data, we find that Kasen International Holdings's ROCE is meaningfully better than the 10% average in the Consumer Durables industry. I think that's good to see, since it implies the company is better than other companies at making the most of its capital. Putting aside its position relative to its industry for now, in absolute terms, Kasen International Holdings's ROCE is currently very good.Kasen International Holdings reported an ROCE of 24% -- better than 3 years ago, when the company didn't make a profit. That implies the business has been improving. ROCE can be misleading for companies in cyclical industries, with returns looking impressive during the boom times, but very weak during the busts. ROCE is, after all, simply a snap shot of a single year. You can check if Kasen International Holdings has cyclical profits by looking at this free graph of past earnings, revenue and cash flow.Do Kasen International Holdings's Current Liabilities Skew Its ROCE?Current liabilities are short term bills and invoices that need to be paid in 12 months or less. The ROCE equation subtracts current liabilities from capital employed, so a company with a lot of current liabilities appears to have less capital employed, and a higher ROCE than otherwise. To counter this, investors can check if a company has high current liabilities relative to total assets.Kasen International Holdings has total assets of CN¥7.0b and current liabilities of CN¥3.3b. Therefore its current liabilities are equivalent to approximately 47% of its total assets. Kasen International Holdings has a medium level of current liabilities, boosting its ROCE somewhat.Story continuesOur Take On Kasen International Holdings's ROCEDespite this, it reports a high ROCE, and may be worth investigating further.
