Specifically, we'll consider its Return On Capital Employed (ROCE), since that will give us an insight into how efficiently the business can generate profits from the capital it requires.Firstly, we'll go over how we calculate ROCE. Second, we'll look at its ROCE compared to similar companies. Finally, we'll look at how its current liabilities affect its ROCE.What is Return On Capital Employed (ROCE)?ROCE is a measure of a company's yearly pre-tax profit (its return), relative to the capital employed in the business. All else being equal, a better business will have a higher ROCE. Overall, it is a valuable metric that has its flaws. Renowned investment researcher Michael Mauboussin has suggested that a high ROCE can indicate that 'one dollar invested in the company generates value of more than one dollar'.How Do You Calculate Return On Capital Employed?The formula for calculating the return on capital employed is:Return on Capital Employed = Earnings Before Interest and Tax (EBIT) ÷ (Total Assets - Current Liabilities)Or for Raute Oyj:0.18 = €10.0m ÷ (€90m - €36m) (Based on the trailing twelve months to September 2019.)So, Raute Oyj has an ROCE of 18%.
See our latest analysis for Raute Oyj Does Raute Oyj Have A Good ROCE?ROCE is commonly used for comparing the performance of similar businesses. In our analysis, Raute Oyj's ROCE is meaningfully higher than the 12% average in the Machinery industry. We would consider this a positive, as it suggests it is using capital more effectively than other similar companies. Setting aside the comparison to its industry for a moment, Raute Oyj's ROCE in absolute terms currently looks quite high.We can see that, Raute Oyj currently has an ROCE of 18%, less than the 27% it reported 3 years ago. Therefore we wonder if the company is facing new headwinds. Companies in cyclical industries can be difficult to understand using ROCE, as returns typically look high during boom times, and low during busts. This is because ROCE only looks at one year, instead of considering returns across a whole cycle. Since the future is so important for investors, you should check out our free report on analyst forecasts for Raute Oyj.Do Raute Oyj's Current Liabilities Skew Its ROCE?Current liabilities are short term bills and invoices that need to be paid in 12 months or less. Due to the way ROCE is calculated, a high level of current liabilities makes a company look as though it has less capital employed, and thus can (sometimes unfairly) boost the ROCE. To counter this, investors can check if a company has high current liabilities relative to total assets.Raute Oyj has total liabilities of €36m and total assets of €90m. As a result, its current liabilities are equal to approximately 40% of its total assets. A medium level of current liabilities boosts Raute Oyj's ROCE somewhat.What We Can Learn From Raute Oyj's ROCEDespite this, it reports a high ROCE, and may be worth investigating further.
