But when you pick a company that is really flourishing, you can make more than 100%. For example, the NCAB Group AB (publ) (STO:NCAB) share price has soared 101% return in just a single year. On top of that, the share price is up 59% in about a quarter. We'll need to follow NCAB Group for a while to get a better sense of its share price trend, since it hasn't been listed for particularly long. Check out our latest analysis for NCAB Group There is no denying that markets are sometimes efficient, but prices do not always reflect underlying business performance. One imperfect but simple way to consider how the market perception of a company has shifted is to compare the change in the earnings per share (EPS) with the share price movement.During the last year NCAB Group grew its earnings per share (EPS) by 118%. This EPS growth is reasonably close to the 101% increase in the share price. So this implies that investor expectations of the company have remained pretty steady. Check if analysts think NCAB Group will grow revenue in the future.What About Dividends?It is important to consider the total shareholder return, as well as the share price return, for any given stock. The TSR incorporates the value of any spin-offs or discounted capital raisings, along with any dividends, based on the assumption that the dividends are reinvested. It's fair to say that the TSR gives a more complete picture for stocks that pay a dividend. As it happens, NCAB Group's TSR for the last year was 108%, which exceeds the share price return mentioned earlier. The dividends paid by the company have thusly boosted the total shareholder return.A Different PerspectiveIt's nice to see that NCAB Group shareholders have gained 108% over the last year , including dividends . A substantial portion of that gain has come in the last three months, with the stock up 59% in that time. This suggests the company is continuing to win over new investors.
