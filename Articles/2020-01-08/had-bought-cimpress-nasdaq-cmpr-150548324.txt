For example, the Cimpress plc (NASDAQ:CMPR) share price is 62% higher than it was five years ago, which is more than the market average. It's also good to see that the stock is up 7.3% in a year. Check out our latest analysis for Cimpress In his essay The Superinvestors of Graham-and-Doddsville Warren Buffett described how share prices do not always rationally reflect the value of a business. One flawed but reasonable way to assess how sentiment around a company has changed is to compare the earnings per share (EPS) with the share price.Over half a decade, Cimpress managed to grow its earnings per share at 16% a year. The EPS growth is more impressive than the yearly share price gain of 10% over the same period. So it seems the market isn't so enthusiastic about the stock these days.The image below shows how EPS has tracked over time (if you click on the image you can see greater detail).NasdaqGS:CMPR Past and Future Earnings, January 7th 2020MoreWe like that insiders have been buying shares in the last twelve months. Maybe the share price is just taking a breather while the business executes on its growth strategy.
