But the changes happening are much more than cosmetic. The store is being completely reimagined with a new name, new look and an exciting new concept. Coming this January: Sleep Solutions Outlet!BMC Mattress, soon to be Sleep Solutions Outlet, is a true outlet. The chain has priority access to Sealy and Stearns & Foster Secondary Bedding products, which include factory seconds, overstocks, floor models, discontinued items and comfort returns. "Because of our unique relationship with the manufacturer, we get access to these truly phenomenal deals," said Mike Souza, Sleep Solutions Outlet CEO. How phenomenal? The chain sells mattresses for up to 60% off MSRP every single day. No sales, no gimmicks, no haggling.Making Mattress buying easy.Stores are being redesigned to make the entire mattress buying experience easy and enjoyable. "We want shoppers to be comfortable during the entire process," Souza explains. "Our sales team is there to educate shoppers about the various mattress technologies and help them choose the right one. We've taken all the pressure and hassle out of buying a mattress."Built on a foundation of trust and transparency. Since opening its first store in 2010, BMC Mattress has become the nation's leading liquidator of high quality, name brand mattresses. With a bold new look and concept, Sleep Solutions Outlet will build on a high level of consumer trust and confidence. "We're proud of the chain we built with BMC," adds Souza, "And we're excited to go even further with Sleep Solutions Outlet."Grand Opening Celebrations planned at all seven Kansas City-area locations.To celebrate the launch of their new name and new look, each store will be offering special grand opening deals and giveaways through February.Media Contact: Jerry Wessels231950@email4pr.com 214.354.3634CisionMoreView original content to download multimedia:http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/big-changes-are-coming-to-bmc-mattress-300982336.htmlSOURCE BMC MattressReblogShareTweetShareRecently ViewedYour list is empty.What to Read NextBiocontainment expert on coronavirus: There's no need for average people to panic yetYahoo FinanceA drug shortage due to the coronavirus is a 'considerable risk:' Fmr.
