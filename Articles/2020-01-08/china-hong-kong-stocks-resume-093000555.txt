The Hang Seng Index rose 0.3 per cent to 28,322.06.Gains in US stocks overnight bolstered sentiment across Asia, with equity gauges from Japan to South Korea rising at least 1 per cent. Gold futures retreated as much as 0.8 per cent from a six-year high on Tuesday, as demand for safe assets faded a bit.Hong Kong's glory days are far from over, says stock exchange boss"The risk appetite returned with a vengeance as investors looked past concerns about escalating tension in the Middle East while focusing on the robust US economic data," said Stephen Innes, a strategist at AxiTrader. "Iran's economic hardships would deter an attack on any oil infrastructure in the sense that such an attack would likely freeze out any existing Iranian exports and put the Iran economy into an even deeper hole."The technology-heavy ChiNext gauge of small-caps in Shenzhen rose to its highest close since April 2018, with a 1.8 per cent gain. A number of factors, such as looser liquidity after a cut in the reserve requirement ratios of banks and earnings growth recovery following massive asset-impairment losses linked with goodwill, could be fuelling its momentum.Beijing steering savings into equities funds will ensure stocks rally, analysts sayWens Foodstuff Group, China's second-largest pig farming company, jumped 6.8 per cent to 38.27 yuan for a seventh straight day of gains, after saying its net income may have surged by as much as 261 per cent from a year earlier in 2019. The rise in its stock also buoyed industry peers. Muyuan Foodstuff climbed 3.8 per cent to 93.80 yuan and Hunan New Wellful added 3.9 per cent to 8.48 yuan.Huayi Brothers Media and other Chinese film studios staged a comeback after a disappointing performance in 2019. China's box office might have risen significantly in the fourth quarter, because of a low base in 2018, according to Central China Securities.Huayi Brothers rose 7.5 per cent to 5.14 yuan, taking its gains this year to 11 per cent. Shanghai New Culture Media jumped by the 10 per cent daily limit on Tuesday to 5.75 yuan, bringing its rally to 41 per cent in 2020, while Wanda Film Holding advanced 4.6 per cent to 21.83 yuan, and was heading for a 20 per cent gain this year.Shares of Chinese film studios took a beating last year amid increased censorship and a crackdown on industry-wide tax evasion, which led to thousands of film studios shutting down.Geely Automobile Holdings climbed 1.6 per cent to HK$15.68 after it revealed positive sales data for December. The Chinese car manufacturer's sales increased by 39 per cent from a low base a year ago, said Toliver Ma, analyst at Guotai Junan Securities.China Evergrande Group lost 2.3 per cent to HK$21.25 after the Wuhan municipal government said six people were killed in an accident at an under-construction tourism resort owned by the property developer.This article originally appeared in the South China Morning Post (SCMP), the most authoritative voice reporting on China and Asia for more than a century.
