"It’s a new way to start your new year."Starbucks’ move comes after the People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals (PETA) became a Starbucks shareholder following several requests for the coffee company to stop charging extra for non-dairy milk.The announcement of the new menu items coincides with "Veganuary," a campaign that aims to get people to adopt a plant-based diet for the year.Other chains like Chipotle, Burger King and Carl's Jr. have been adding more plant-based options to their menus, with products developed by Beyond Meat and Impossible Foods.
