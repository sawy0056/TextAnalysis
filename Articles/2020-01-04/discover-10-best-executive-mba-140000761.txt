Executive MBA programs supply individuals with the skill set they want from a traditional MBA program but with increased flexibility and leadership training. These programs are typically designed for business leaders who already have an abundance of impressive work experience but who wish to prepare themselves for higher-level executive positions. Here are the top 10 executive MBA programs as ranked by U.S. News.10. Massachusetts Institute of Technology (Sloan)Location: Cambridge, MassachusettsOverall Best Business Schools rank: 3 (tie)Fact: According to MIT's website for its EMBA program, the program is intended for "mid-career executives poised at pivotal junctures in their careers." The program lasts for 20 months, with classes occurring every three weeks on Fridays and Saturdays. In addition, there is a one-week course module every six months and a weeklong international project.Learn more about the Sloan School of Management.9. New York University (Stern)Location: New York CityOverall Best Business Schools rank: 12 (tie)Fact: Executive MBA students at NYU select 12 elective classes in their second year, which means that they can customize their coursework. They also can elect to concentrate on one business discipline or specialize in two or three business fields simultaneously. Potential specializations vary widely, from financial instruments and markets to supply chain management and global sourcing to entrepreneurship and innovation.Learn more about the Leonard N. Stern School of Business.8. University of California--Los Angeles (Anderson)Location: Los AngelesOverall Best Business Schools rank: 16Fact: According to UCLA, two goals of its executive MBA program are to prepare students to shoulder greater responsibility in the workplace and accelerate their careers. The school's 22-month EMBA curriculum is meant to cultivate skills that are necessary for advancement, its website states.Learn more about the Anderson School of Management.7. University of California--Berkeley (Haas)Location: Berkeley, CaliforniaOverall Best Business Schools rank: 6 (tie)Fact: A quarter of the EMBA coursework at Haas involves experiential learning, according to the school. The EMBA curriculum includes various off-campus field immersions where students have an opportunity to hone their business skills, from Silicon Valley and Washington, D.C., to Sau Paulo and Shanghai.Learn more about the Haas School of Business.6. Duke University (Fuqua) (NC)Location: Durham, North CarolinaOverall Best Business Schools rank: 10 (tie)Fact: Duke offers a global EMBA program and a weekend EMBA program. The global option lasts 18 months and includes weeklong residencies in Asia, Latin America and Europe, in addition to courses at Duke's Durham campus. The 19-month weekend program is designed to minimize time away from work and family, so students usually meet once a month in Durham and complete one three-day weekend residency each month.Learn more about the Fuqua School of Business.5. University of Michigan--Ann Arbor (Ross)Location: Ann Arbor, MichiganOverall Best Business Schools rank: 10 (tie)Fact: This B-school urges prospective EMBA students to send a resume to an admissions officer prior to submitting an application so that the official can provide guidance on whether the program is a good fit. On the EMBA application requirements section of its website, the school notes that its EMBA students usually have at least 10 years of "progressive, full-time work experience," including at least five years as a manager or supervisor.Learn more about the Stephen M. Ross School of Business.4. Columbia University (NY)Location: New YorkOverall Best Business Schools rank: 6 (tie)Fact: During the first academic year of the Columbia EMBA program, students are divided into clusters, which are peer groups with 30 to 70 classmates who complete all of their core courses together. Within those clusters are smaller groups called "learning teams" that include five or six students with diverse backgrounds. The program's structure is intended to encourage cross-disciplinary cooperation.Story continuesLearn more about Columbia Business School.3. University of Pennsylvania (Wharton)Location: PhiladelphiaOverall Best Business Schools rank: 1Fact: Wharton executive MBA students are eligible for executive career coaching services provided by the school. They must receive an endorsement from their employer for time off from work to participate in the 24-month program, which is residential on alternating weekends. The program includes a one-week global experience, and about a third of each cohort is international.Learn more about the Wharton School.2. Northwestern University (Kellogg) (IL)Location: Evanston, IllinoisOverall Best Business Schools rank: 6 (tie)Fact: Kellogg executive MBA students have the choice of going to class twice a month at the school's Evanston campus or once a month at its Miami campus. The Evanston option has four intensive weeks while the Miami option has three. Students can opt to begin either two-year program in January or September and primarily are in class on the weekends.Learn more about the Kellogg School of Management.1. University of Chicago (Booth)Location: ChicagoOverall Best Business Schools rank: 3 (tie)Fact: Chicago's program dates back to 1943 and claims to be the world's first executive MBA program. The 21-month program accepts 240 experienced professionals each June. Because of its difficulty, all applicants must take either the Executive Assessment administered by the Graduate Management Admission Council or an equivalent entrance exam such as the GMAT or GRE.Learn more about the Booth School of Business.Learn more about the best business schools.Get advice on applying to business school, and explore the 2020 Best Business Schools rankings.
