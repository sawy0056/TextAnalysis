But as Warren Buffett has mused, 'If you've been playing poker for half an hour and you still don't know who the patsy is, you're the patsy.' When they buy such story stocks, investors are all too often the patsy.So if you're like me, you might be more interested in profitable, growing companies, like Secure Trust Bank (LON:STB). While profit is not necessarily a social good, it's easy to admire a business that can consistently produce it. In comparison, loss making companies act like a sponge for capital - but unlike such a sponge they do not always produce something when squeezed. See our latest analysis for Secure Trust Bank How Fast Is Secure Trust Bank Growing?The market is a voting machine in the short term, but a weighing machine in the long term, so share price follows earnings per share (EPS) eventually. It's no surprise, then, that I like to invest in companies with EPS growth. Over the last three years, Secure Trust Bank has grown EPS by 12% per year. That growth rate is fairly good, assuming the company can keep it up.One way to double-check a company's growth is to look at how its revenue, and earnings before interest and tax (EBIT) margins are changing. I note that Secure Trust Bank's revenue from operations was lower than its revenue in the last twelve months, so that could distort my analysis of its margins. While we note Secure Trust Bank's EBIT margins were flat over the last year, revenue grew by a solid 18% to UK£127m. That's a real positive.You can take a look at the company's revenue and earnings growth trend, in the chart below. So why not check this interactive chart depicting future EPS estimates, for Secure Trust Bank?Are Secure Trust Bank Insiders Aligned With All Shareholders?Like that fresh smell in the air when the rains are coming, insider buying fills me with optimistic anticipation. That's because insider buying often indicates that those closest to the company have confidence that the share price will perform well. However, small purchases are not always indicative of conviction, and insiders don't always get it right.We haven't seen any insiders selling Secure Trust Bank shares, in the last year. So it's definitely nice that Non-Executive Chairman Michael Forsyth bought UK£6.7k worth of shares at an average price of around UK£13.47.Is Secure Trust Bank Worth Keeping An Eye On?One important encouraging feature of Secure Trust Bank is that it is growing profits. While some companies are struggling to grow EPS, Secure Trust Bank seems free from that morose affliction. The cherry on top is that we have an insider buying shares. That encourages me further to keep an eye on this stock. Of course, just because Secure Trust Bank is growing does not mean it is undervalued.
