On the other hand, we'd be remiss not to mention that insider sales have been known to precede tough periods for a business. So shareholders might well want to know whether insiders have been buying or selling shares in Financial Institutions, Inc. (NASDAQ:FISI).What Is Insider Buying?It is perfectly legal for company insiders, including board members, to buy and sell stock in a company. However, most countries require that the company discloses such transactions to the market.We don't think shareholders should simply follow insider transactions. But it is perfectly logical to keep tabs on what insiders are doing. For example, a Harvard University study found that 'insider purchases earn abnormal returns of more than 6% per year. See our latest analysis for Financial Institutions Financial Institutions Insider Transactions Over The Last YearOver the last year, we can see that the biggest insider purchase was by Senior VP and Chief Customer Experience & Go to Market Officer of Five Star Bank Joseph Dugan for US$302k worth of shares, at about US$28.45 per share. That implies that an insider found the current price of US$31.67 per share to be enticing. Of course they may have changed their mind. But this suggests they are optimistic. While we always like to see insider buying, it's less meaningful if the purchases were made at much lower prices, as the opportunity they saw may have passed. In this case we're pleased to report that the insider purchases were made at close to current prices.Financial Institutions insiders may have bought shares in the last year, but they didn't sell any. Usually, the higher the insider ownership, the more likely it is that insiders will be incentivised to build the company for the long term. It appears that Financial Institutions insiders own 4.5% of the company, worth about US$23m. While this is a strong but not outstanding level of insider ownership, it's enough to indicate some alignment between management and smaller shareholders.So What Do The Financial Institutions Insider Transactions Indicate?The fact that there have been no Financial Institutions insider transactions recently certainly doesn't bother us. On a brighter note, the transactions over the last year are encouraging. Insiders own shares in Financial Institutions and we see no evidence to suggest they are worried about the future.
