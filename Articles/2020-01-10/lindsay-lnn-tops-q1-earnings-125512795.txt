A quarter ago, it was expected that this irrigation equipment maker would post earnings of $0.34 per share when it actually produced earnings of $0.54, delivering a surprise of 58.82%.Over the last four quarters, the company has surpassed consensus EPS estimates two times.Lindsay, which belongs to the Zacks Manufacturing - Farm Equipment industry, posted revenues of $109.39 million for the quarter ended November 2019, missing the Zacks Consensus Estimate by 2.71%. This compares to year-ago revenues of $111.95 million. The company has not been able to beat consensus revenue estimates over the last four quarters.The sustainability of the stock's immediate price movement based on the recently-released numbers and future earnings expectations will mostly depend on management's commentary on the earnings call.Lindsay shares have added about 0.7% since the beginning of the year versus the S&P 500's gain of 0.7%.What's Next for Lindsay?While Lindsay has outperformed the market so far this year, the question that comes to investors' minds is: what's next for the stock?There are no easy answers to this key question, but one reliable measure that can help investors address this is the company's earnings outlook.
