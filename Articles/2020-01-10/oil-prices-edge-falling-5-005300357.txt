But he did not speak of a counterattack, easing concerns of further escalation of conflict between the two nations.Last week, Iran’s top general Qasem Soleimani was killed in a U.S. airstrike in Baghdad.Iran has vowed to retaliate following the attack.Meanwhile, the Energy Information Administration (EIA) reported that U.S. crude stockpiles rose by 1.2 million barrels for the week ended Jan. 3. The market was looking for a decline of 3.6 million barrels, according to analyst forecasts compiled by Investing.com.Gasoline inventories soared by 9.1 million barrels, compared with expectations for a rise of 2.7 million barrels, the EIA said. Distillate stockpiles climbed by 5.3 million barrels, versus forecasts for a build of 3.9 million barrels.
