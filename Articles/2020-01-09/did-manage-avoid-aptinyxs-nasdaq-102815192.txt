View our latest analysis for Aptinyx Given that Aptinyx didn't make a profit in the last twelve months, we'll focus on revenue growth to form a quick view of its business development. Generally speaking, companies without profits are expected to grow revenue every year, and at a good clip. That's because it's hard to be confident a company will be sustainable if revenue growth is negligible, and it never makes a profit.In just one year Aptinyx saw its revenue fall by 44%. That looks pretty grim, at a glance. The market obviously agrees, since the share price tanked 80%. That's a stern reminder that profitless companies need to grow the top line, at the very least.
