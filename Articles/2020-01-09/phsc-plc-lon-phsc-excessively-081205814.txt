This analysis aims first to contrast CEO compensation with other companies that have similar market capitalization. After that, we will consider the growth in the business. The aim of all this is to consider the appropriateness of CEO pay levels. View our latest analysis for PHSC How Does Stephen King's Compensation Compare With Similar Sized Companies?At the time of writing, our data says that PHSC plc has a market cap of UK£2.1m, and reported total annual CEO compensation of UK£96k for the year to March 2019. While we always look at total compensation first, we note that the salary component is less, at UK£90k. We took a group of companies with market capitalizations below UK£152m, and calculated the median CEO total compensation to be UK£252k.Most shareholders would consider it a positive that Stephen King takes less total compensation than the CEOs of most similar size companies, leaving more for shareholders. While this is a good thing, you'll need to understand the business better before you can form an opinion.The graphic below shows how CEO compensation at PHSC has changed from year to year.AIM:PHSC CEO Compensation, January 8th 2020MoreIs PHSC plc Growing?Over the last three years PHSC plc has grown its earnings per share (EPS) by an average of 93% per year (using a line of best fit). Its revenue is down 26% over last year.This demonstrates that the company has been improving recently. A good result. Revenue growth is a real positive for growth, but ultimately profits are more important. We don't have analyst forecasts, but shareholders might want to examine this detailed historical graph of earnings, revenue and cash flow.Has PHSC plc Been A Good Investment?PHSC plc has generated a total shareholder return of 14% over three years, so most shareholders would be reasonably content. But they probably don't want to see the CEO paid more than is normal for companies around the same size.In Summary...It looks like PHSC plc pays its CEO less than similar sized companies.Many would consider this to indicate that the pay is modest since the business is growing. While returns over the last few years haven't been top notch, there is nothing to suggest to us that Stephen King is overcompensated. It's great to see a company that pays its CEO reasonably, even while growing. But it would be nice if insiders were also buying shares.
