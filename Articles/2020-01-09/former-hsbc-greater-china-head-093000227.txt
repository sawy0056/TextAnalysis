She will oversee all banking relationships with companies, and handle two product units " cash management and trade, and the investment banking business.HSBC, Spark Alliance and the law behind the latest fury in Hong Kong protestsThe veteran banker, who has 35 years of experience, has helped many of HSBC's mainland Chinese customers expand their businesses in Southeast Asia to escape the impact of the US-China trade war. She is seen as an expert on the Southeast Asian region, according to analysts.Wong will report to OCBC group chief executive officer Samuel Tsien.Wong's move to OCBC Bank is something of a homecoming. She started her career with OCBC Bank in 1984 when she joined as a trainee, and a year later was made the bank's first China desk manager, based in its Hong Kong branch, according to OCBC.Trade war manufacturing exodus creates boom times for Chinese logistics companiesWong joined HSBC in 1992 and worked until August last year when she "decided to leave HSBC to pursue an external opportunity", according to a memo sent at the time by her boss, Peter Wong Tung-shun, deputy chairman and chief executive of Asia-Pacific, to all 47,000 staff in the Greater China region which she oversaw.Wong's departure shocked the market as it came in the same week that group chief executive John Flint left after 18 months on the job. Wong was not replaced at HSBC because the group has four CEOs in each of Hong Kong, Macau, Taiwan and mainland China.Founded in 1932, OCBC Bank is the longest established Singapore bank. It is the second largest financial firm in Southeast Asia by assets, with 560 branches and a representative office in each of 19 countries including Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia and Greater China.
