Increasing use of advanced healthcare technologies for better understanding of patients and minimization of risk during the actual surgical procedure is boosting the demand for 3D printed surgical models.Grand_View_Research_LogoMoreThe introduction of synthetic modeling of human anatomy has made a significant leap forward. The 3D printed surgical models allow preadoption of surgical instruments by improving the 3D perception of the planned operation. The technology also enables surgeons to envision, practice, and then perform the actual surgery for saving time and increasing precision. These models can also be used to perform mock surgeries, thereby improving the prediction of the outcomes.Key suggestions from the report:The orthopedic surgery segment accounted for highest revenue in 2018 as the 3D models can assist in pre-operative planning by defining the geometry of bone loss, tumor mass, and nearby vessels to determine the most appropriate osteotomy site along with most appropriate prosthesis The neurosurgery segment is anticipated to witness the fastest CAGR of 11.3% over the forecast period.
