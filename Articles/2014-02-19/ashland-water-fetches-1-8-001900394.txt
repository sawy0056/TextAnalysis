Covington, Ky.-based chemicals and plastics maker Ashland (ASH) agreed to sell its water chemicals business, Ashland Water Technologies, to private equity firm Clayton, Dubilier & Rice for about $1.8 bil so it can focus on its core businesses. The water chemicals business, with annual revenue of about $1.7 bil, employs about 3,000 and makes chemicals used in paper, energy and food, and other industries. The transaction is expected to close by Sept. 30. Ashland fell 1.2% to 56.19.
